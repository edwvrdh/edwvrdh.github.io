Introduction

Active Garden is a networked technology consisting of a mobile application and an automatic watering system that encourages the elderly to keep fit and maintain their plants. Users complete at-home workouts and are rewarded with their plants being watered. Workouts are customisable, and plants are registered within the app, where the correct measure of water is inputted for each individual plant.


The aim of this project was to design a product that promotes the concept of active aging to the elderly through networked technologies. In doing so, such individuals maintain positive health, participation, and security in their daily life, which is integral to leading a fulfilling and enjoyable lifestyle in elderhood. Within the scope of this project, it was required that a combination of two networked technologies were to be used to address the problem statement. Only one device/user interface was required to be focused on when prototyping, with the general concept of how it will communicate and connect with the second device being mandatory to be explored.


With a team of four members, I participated in conducting interviews to gather user research, creating a storyboard, a paper prototype and a high-fidelity prototype of the design idea, as well as guiding a participant through user testing evaluation.

Key Interview Findings


Key Insight #1

Concerns for long-term health

Participants acknowledged the degradation of their health as a result of aging, primarily outlining physical challenges such as decreased mobility.




Key Insight #2

Gardening is a popular hobby

Gardening helps with improving the mental and physical health of elderly people. Those that did not have their own garden often stated they would like to do so in the near future.




Key Insight #3

Elderly people like to participate in social groups

Elderly people often participate in social or community groups that relate to their hobbies.




With this user research, it was decided that the proposed technology for active aging would revolve around gardening due to its involvement in the majority of elderly lives, as well as incorporating physical activity to tackle long-term health concerns.




Storyboarding



To better visualise and understand how the proposed technology will be used in the context of an eldery person's home, I created a storyboard that depicts a typical interaction with Active Garden. By visually sharing a hypothetical situation of the target audience, the team was able to share a common understanding of how the technology would function and its design directions.




Prototyping

With the target audience being elderly people, it was essential that the technology was designed in a manner that was easy to learn. With this in mind, many of the elements within the prototypes are consistent with the user interfaces of many other applications, with a specific focus on utilising icons and visual cues to navigate through the app.
